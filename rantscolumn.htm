Article: Community Slowdown: A look at the direction of the fandom

 

Ever since the end of Beast Machines it seems like the TF Community, as a whole has started to slow down. Its like we have hit a pot hole, and where stuck, not knowing what to do. With the return of vehicle Transformers in late 2001, it seemed like Hasbro was running out of ideas, it was basically like they put a new spin of G1, by putting G1 in a pretty new package and sent it out for us to "buy". Now don't get me wrong I loved the return of vehicles, in RID, but the somehow it felt like there was a "hole", if you want to say, left in the Community. By this I mean that at the end of the Beast Era, I had to say goodbye to the characters that got me back into Transformers. Yes I know the toys for BM, where well below par; compared to BW, but BM was special. And in my opinion was one of the best layout, and displayed series in the entire TF community, American, and Japanese. The way Megatron came off, as just pure evil, obliterating every Transformer on Cybertron, taking over the planet, and leaving the Good guys thinking, hey we may not win this one. I loved how at least this one time Evil, actually had a chance to win. And yes I was pulling for Megatron at the End of BM. His character came full circle at the end. Where in the beginning he was leading a ragtag group devious Predicons plotting to over through him around every corner. Then he evolved. At the End of BM, he had everything perfect. One central being making ALL the choices. Not everyone trying to work to over through him, or work to obliterate the Maximals, just Megatron the Generals (who he had full control over) and millions of drones. I remember at the beginning of BW thinking that the Predicons has no chance, Primal will waste all of you. But as the Beast Era continued I remember thinking that, evil might win this one, and it would be cool if they did. And since that show I haven�t had a feeling like that. It like every time I watched RID or Armada I knew that the Autobots are going to win; I just get this feeling of "oh yeah I know the good guys are going to win" there is no complexity left in the shows. Now I know that RID and especially Armada were/are intended to target little kids, but the need some sort of complexity, in order to keep the older fans happy with the product.

Then with RID, yeah the character were ok, the toys in my opinion were good, and the show ok. But I remember thinking oh wait this episode reminds me of a G1 episode, or a MasterForce episode. It was almost like they were starting to run out of ideas when the creators of the show were writing it. Bringing back the Headmaster, bringing back the Beast as Predicons. Yeah it was nice to see Fort Max on TV again, but if I wanted to see the real Fort Max I will throw in a Headmasters tape and watch that. Using old molds for the toys, which in some ways was nice, but in some degree, subtracted for the overall product. I wanted mostly newer stuff; when I saw those gobots, and the Beast repaints, and the Japanese Max repaints, I was thinking, oh yeah I already have those. And I know that Takara probably did that to help save money, but it came off a little cheap. The characters themselves on a level of complexity were poorly constructed, but on appearance were ok. What I mean is that Prime was always sitting back at the base waiting for the bad guys to make the first move, he never took the battle to them(except at the end), but we had to sit thought 37 episodes of the bad guys bringing it. In the last episodes the Autobots started to step their game up little but by then it was too late, we already new the outcome. And it was disappointing seeing a show that could have some much potential, in my opinion do a mediocre performance.

Then came Armada.

When I first saw the toys for Armada back in February of 2002 I started thinking, man those sure look like little kids toys. But as the release date for Armada came closer and I started seeing the final production pictures I started thinking that this could have potential. With the Minicon gimmick and the possibilities involved with that. But when I bought my first Armada toy, Optimus Prime, and removed him from the packaging I said to myself, its a shame that such a good toy line has come to no articulation, and no detail. About this time Armada started to air on Cartoon Network. I remember think holy crap this show sucks, compared to every past show that was put out under the Transformers name. The animation was horrible, and the character designs made me sick, but there was a constant, the "complexless" story lines and the knowledge of the Autobots always wining were still there. As the show rolled on I stopped watching it. Then came the mini Prime with Overrun. Those toys changed my perspective on Armada, hey maybe these toys could be actually cool. But in my mind the show itself still was lacking below my expectations. Finally came the toy that I had waited for ever since I had seen TFTM, Unicron. That particular toy made me tune back in to Armada, hoping to see him on TV again. I know that Armada is making huge bucks for Hasbro and everyone involved, but they have received very little of my money. And I was happy. Even thought the show still had the complexless story lines, annoying kids, horrible character architecture, horrible animation, and so on and so forth. I had at least, for that time made my peace with the show.

As I look into the future of what possibilities await us for the Transformers Universe I see the same thing repeating it self over again. Energon, look like it will have some decent toys, but the rest aren�t looking to pretty, and again a less the par show, but this all remains to be seen. Its like we have hit a huge pot hole on the road of the Transformers history, and we will keep spinning in that hole until something changes in the layout of the storylines, character development, quality, and so on and so forth. There is however a simple answer to all of this. It�s called two little sub lines that are called Transformers Universe, and Transformers Alternators.

The Universe line has endless possibilities if it were to be made into a show; incorporating all the "Universes" in Transformers history into one. Not separate communities/separate �Universes� like we have now. But a conclusion to all that is Transformers History. And then from there move on. Reinvent Transformers, do something new. We were left with holes that need to be filled in Universe could potentially do this, with the right direction and the right show. Thus bringing the complexity back into the show, and the Fandom, and picking up were we left off with previous shows. Even though the toys themselves are all repaints Hasbro could make new character, in new mold that we haven�t seen yet. But I foresee that not happening and just like the WWF/E and the Sports Entertainment industry in whole, Transformers will fade into the pages of History of pop culture in about 5-10 years. Unless something is done now.
