The Cybertron Info Core was launched into the World Wide Web on May 17, 1998, at that time it was called The Cybertron HardDrive. The Hard drive was on-line for about seven months, but because of lack of updates and a poor layout, I took it off the web, and started designing my next endevour, The Cybertron MainFrame. The Cybertron Mainframe was on-line for about three months. It too was taken down because of lack of updates; it would be seven months, before I started to even think about a new website.

Flash forward to September 25 1999, Beast Machines is in it first season and that Saturday "Master of The House" premires. During the broadcast I make plans for my new website; The Cybertron Info Core.

The first version is created on Microsoft Frontpage and launch to the web on October 3 1999. It is on-line until February 2000. In February I moved web hosting sites from Angelfire.com to Geocities.com. The site was updated a few times a month, it was updated about every two or three months, eventually the site became static and I let it fade into the webpages of Transformers history...

In late 2001 I got Dreamwaver 4.0 as a "gift", and I put it to work, I started to develope my new site "Primus Designs". Primus Designs is my own unofficial company that devolopes websites using HTML, XML, DHTML, and JAVASCRIPT. The company also does computer repair and home networking. In November 2001 one of my friends asked my how the "CIC" was coming, I said it was dead, he asked why, I told him that it beacem staitic. He quiclkly told me to to get it running again, that it was a good idea. Thankfully I listened to him, and in December the site got rehauled and on Christmas Day the world experienced "The Cybertron Info Core 2.0". The CIC 2.0 had a good layout but there were alot of code problems. The CIC experienced several months of downtime in 2002, and several "tweaks" over the months. I though about closing the whole site but I decided to give it one more try.

On October 12, 2002 "The Cybertron Info Core 3.0" was launch. The newist incarnation includes a easier to use interface, Flash objects, and enhanced features. The CIC 3.0 breaks the "norm" for news updates; this means that every Saturday the site puts out the previous weeks worth of Transformers news on its mainpage. So if on any day you can't find last weeks, or last months news you can visit the CIC and it will be there.

Over the years the CIC has had many different versions and names, along with breaking news and many exclusives. I know that this site will stay active for sometime, and continue to serve as a popular website.
